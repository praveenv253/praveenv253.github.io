Well, for a while I'd been wondering what Monte Carlo simulations are all about. It never really struck me to wiki it up until recently. On doing so, I was rather struck by the relative ease of its implementation (at least, for simplistic computations) so I decided to actually go ahead and write a program for it.

The program in question is one that attempts to estimate the value of $ \pi $. The figure that inspired me to try this out can also be found on the wiki page. In short, here's the way it works:
  1. Create a bunch of random points in the range $[0,1) \times [0,1)$ uniformly
  2. See which ones of them fall inside the unit circle and which ones don't
  3. Quite clearly, the ratio of the two numbers gives you the ratio of the two areas (since the distribution of random numbers is uniform!)
  4. Since the area of the unit circle is simply $ \pi $, the ratio we just procured is one-fourth the value of $ \pi $, and therefore we need to multiply it by 4 to get the actual value.
